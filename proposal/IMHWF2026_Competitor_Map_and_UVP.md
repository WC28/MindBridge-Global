# IMHWF 2026 Competitor Map and Unique Value Proposition

## Purpose

This note helps the team answer a critical judge question:

**"Has this already been built?"**

The honest answer is:

**Yes, parts of it already exist. No, the full combination is still not well covered.**

That is the strongest position for the project. It means the proposal is not unrealistic, but it still occupies a meaningful gap.

## As Of Date

This map reflects a review completed on **4 July 2026**.

## Short Answer For Presentation

Existing platforms already cover **pieces** of our idea:

- professional networking for healthcare workers
- mental health training and capacity building
- mental health shared decision support
- AI-supported mental health tools

However, we have not identified one widely established platform that combines all of the following in one WHO-aligned, workflow-safe model:

- mental-health-specific focus
- verified professional-only access
- cross-country collaboration
- multidisciplinary case and care coordination
- hospital-to-community continuity tracking
- community follow-up workflow
- privacy-preserving service learning and research layer

## Competitor Map

| Platform | What it already does | Why it matters | Where the gap still exists compared with MindBridge Global |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Doximity** | Verified network for healthcare professionals in the U.S.; collaboration and workflow tools | Shows that clinicians will use a trusted professional network if it saves time | Not mental-health-specific, not built around hospital-to-community continuity, and not designed as a global community mental health coordination model |
| **Sermo** | Global physician-first online community with over 1.3 million HCPs across 150 countries | Proves cross-country clinician networking is already viable | Primarily a professional community and engagement platform, not a continuity-of-care or mental health pathway hub |
| **WHO Academy** | Free global health learning platform with verified course offerings | Shows WHO-aligned global training ecosystems already exist | Training is not the same as a shared multidisciplinary care coordination and follow-up platform |
| **WHO mhGAP** | Global program to scale mental, neurological, and substance use services, especially in LMICs | Strong policy and implementation alignment for the team's idea | mhGAP is a framework and program, not a professional collaboration platform with workflow, case continuity, and operational dashboards |
| **CommonGround** | Mental health software supporting shared decision-making and psychiatric appointment preparation | Strong example that mental-health-specific digital workflow tools can improve real care encounters | More focused on appointment preparation and shared decision-making than cross-site collaboration, discharge follow-up, and international professional networking |
| **Wysa** | AI-enabled mental health support, referral support, between-session care, and healthcare integration | Shows that AI-assisted mental health support can scale when safety and human oversight are explicit | Mainly focused on digital support delivery and AI-enabled care pathways, not a verified professional collaboration network for mental health teams |
| **aves Mental Health / former Global Mental Health Peer Network** | International lived-experience and advocacy network with virtual communication and empowerment structures | Shows there is demand for global mental health networking and capacity building | Focus is advocacy and experts-by-experience, not multidisciplinary service workflow, role-based coordination, or discharge/community continuity |

## What This Means Strategically

The team should **not** claim:

- "No one has ever done anything like this."
- "We invented a social network for mental health professionals."
- "We are replacing hospital systems."

Those claims are too broad and are easy to challenge.

The team **should** claim:

- "Important adjacent models already exist, which shows this direction is viable."
- "What is still missing is a safe, mental-health-specific continuity and collaboration layer."
- "Our innovation is the integration of workflow, multidisciplinary teamwork, community follow-up, and privacy-preserving learning."

## The White Space

The strongest white space for the project is this:

**A verified, mental-health-specific collaboration and continuity platform that sits between clinical work, psychosocial care, discharge planning, and community follow-up without trying to replace the hospital record system.**

This framing is strong because it is:

- specific
- credible
- useful to judges
- safe from overclaiming

## Unique Value Proposition

### Recommended UVP

**MindBridge Global is a safe mental health continuity and collaboration layer that helps multidisciplinary teams coordinate care, follow patients into the community, and turn routine service data into learning without replacing existing hospital systems.**

### Shorter Version

**Not a new hospital record. A new continuity layer for mental health care.**

### Presentation Version

**Other platforms connect professionals, deliver training, or support mental health care in separate pieces. MindBridge Global brings those missing pieces together into one safe, human-supervised workflow for continuity of care.**

## Why This Is Defensible

The originality of the project is not based on inventing every feature from zero. It is based on combining features that are usually separated:

- professional collaboration
- psychosocial workflow
- risk review under human oversight
- discharge and referral continuity
- community follow-up
- privacy-preserving service intelligence

In innovation judging, this is often stronger than an abstract "brand new app" claim because it shows:

- real need
- implementation logic
- lower adoption risk
- practical relevance to mental health systems

## Recommended Positioning Statement

Use this if a judge asks whether similar platforms already exist:

**Yes, adjacent platforms exist for networking, training, or digital mental health support. What remains underbuilt is a mental-health-specific continuity platform that safely connects multidisciplinary teamwork, discharge follow-up, community coordination, and learning data in one model. That is the gap our project addresses.**
