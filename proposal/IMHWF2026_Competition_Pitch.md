# IMHWF 2026 Competition Pitch Sheet

## Recommended Project Title

**MindBridge Global: A Human-Centered Digital Ecosystem for Continuous, Community-Connected Mental Health Care**

## One-Sentence Positioning

MindBridge Global transforms a practical low-cost mental health workflow prototype into a future-ready, WHO-aligned model that connects check-in, professional risk review, multidisciplinary care planning, community follow-up, and privacy-preserving service intelligence.

## 60-Second Pitch

Mental health systems everywhere face the same problem: too many people need help, too few professionals are available, services are fragmented, and patients are forced to repeat their story many times across disconnected settings. Our project, MindBridge Global, responds to this challenge with a human-centered digital ecosystem that supports structured check-in, professional risk review, multidisciplinary coordination, discharge planning, and community follow-up in one continuous workflow.

What makes this different is that it is not just an app idea. We already have a Phase 1 working prototype built with affordable tools, and we are reframing it as a scalable global model aligned with WHO priorities for community-based care, stronger mental health information systems, and ethical digital innovation. The goal is not to replace professionals with AI, but to help teams respond earlier, coordinate better, reduce repetitive workload, and generate better evidence for care improvement.

## Why This Can Win

- It solves a globally relevant problem.
- It is innovative but realistic.
- It starts from a real prototype, not only a concept.
- It aligns clearly with WHO and SDG priorities.
- It is scalable from low-resource settings to multi-site international systems.
- It treats ethics, privacy, and human rights as core design features.

## Key Global Alignment

- **WHO Mental Health Action Plan 2013-2030**: community-based care, prevention, information systems, evidence, and research
- **WHO World Mental Health Report**: strengthen systems, reshape environments, and increase commitment to mental health
- **SDG 3.4**: promote mental health and well-being
- **SDG 3.8**: strengthen continuity within universal health coverage
- **SDG 3.c**: support the health workforce
- **SDG 17**: partnerships and better data for system improvement

## Three Messages To Emphasize In Presentation

1. **This is a continuity-of-care model, not just a digital form system.**
2. **This is human-supervised innovation, not AI replacing care teams.**
3. **This is globally adaptable because it starts low-cost but scales responsibly.**

## Strong Closing Line

**The future of mental health care is not only better treatment, but better connection: between patients and teams, hospitals and communities, and frontline services and the evidence needed to improve them.**
